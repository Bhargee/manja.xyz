About

I am currently a senior undergraduate studying mathematics and computer science at the University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign. I work with Prof. Tim Bretl's research group developing autonomous drone systems for construction site monitoring. I am currently doing my undergraduate thesis work with his group, developing machine learning and machine vision approaches to automatic grip selection for anthropomorphic prosthetic hands. I also work with the Formal Systems Lab/Runtime Verification, applying cutting edge formal verification to vehicle ECU networks.

Interests

With regards to research, I am generally interested in robotic intelligence and autonomy, focusing on vision and learning. I have recently become interested in applications of neuroscience to artificial intelligence, and am also fascinated with programming languages and their implementation. Outside of school and research, I enjoy reading, learning the bass, biking, and boxing. I am also interested in anti-authoritarian and radical politics.
